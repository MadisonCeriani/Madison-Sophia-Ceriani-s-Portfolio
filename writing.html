Writing Artifacts

Five writing artifacts with explanatory paragraphs and embedded PDF viewing space for each project.

Artifact 01

Poppin Joe's Style Guide

This style guide establishes writing and visual standards tailored to Poppin Joe's brand voice. It focuses on consistency across headings, image treatments, and copy choices so future content remains cohesive and recognizable. The Poppin Joe’s Style Guide utilizes rhetoric to justify changes to an established brand so that it can reach a larger demographic. Writing for digital media is different from writing designed for other mediums. Research papers can have long blocks of text because the audience is typically students and a professor. Writing for an audience with a short attention span means the content included must be evaluated more heavily. People skim webpages for relevant info, so online documents must be scannable. When designing the style guide for Poppin Joe’s, successful gourmet popcorn websites were considered as well as research on effective design. In the end the audience was convinced that the new design would be an improvement.

Artifact 02

Freelance Comprehensive Editing Project

This project involved writing for an audience outside the classroom. This document’s audience includes a client with a stake in the finished product. The objective was to edit a travel guide designed for a client looking to visit Italy created by a travel agent. This was a very research heavy project. Reputable online sources provided the information that guided the verification process. Every image was reverse image searched and through that process some discrepancies with the text and images became clear. Any image that was incorrectly labeled was rectified. Editing is more than checking for spelling errors, it is ensuring that the document is as readable and effective as it can be. After an extensive editing process the audience for this project was very pleased by the finished product.

Artifact 04

Personas and Storytelling

This project develops a complete persona and associated narrative to model user-centered communication. It combines research framing with storytelling techniques to make audience motivations clear and actionable. The persona and story detail the life of Julia Sterns. The class material gave me an idea of where to start in terms of designing a character and from there I relied on research. One thing that is very important when creating a persona/storytelling is justifying your choices. Anyone can create a name and randomly attach traits and interests. A successful persona has justifiable elements. When creating the persona, once the age, gender, and profession I had more room to be creative when writing her biography. I used a stock image for her photo, then built a realistic depiction of a woman born in 1982, working in graphic design. Personas are important because when designing a product you have to tailor it to the needs of your audience and personas help fine tune that process.

Artifact 05

Report on Intellectual Property

This report outlines key distinctions in intellectual property and copyright coverage, including practical examples. It was designed to make legal concepts more approachable for creators, students, and small organizations.Intellectual property is imperative to consider when creating content for a brand. It protects the brand identity and prevents competitors from copying. This report exemplifies the importance of Intellectual Property by detailing what is and isn’t covered under copyright, what qualifies as infringement, and examples of those concepts. Research was the most important element when creating this artifact. Through research cases that reflect the definitions of copyright. In the presentation the Nosferatu legal debacle is displayed that even if two projects aka, Nosferatu and Dracula share very little similarity it can still be infringement.An idea cannot be copyrighted but the expression of the idea can be.
