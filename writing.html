Writing Artifacts

Five writing artifacts with explanatory paragraphs and embedded PDF viewing space for each project.

Artifact 01

Poppin Joe's Style Guide

This style guide establishes clear writing and visual standards for Poppin Joe’s, ensuring consistency across typography, imagery, and copy to increase consistency, and brand recognition. With a strong focus on a digitally oriented audience-focused design, the guide emphasizes scannable, concise content that aligns with how users engage with digital media. By prioritizing clarity and readability, it adapts the brand’s message to better meet the expectations of a target demographic. Through research into successful gourmet popcorn brands and effective design practices, the guide provides strategic updates that expand the brand’s appeal to a larger audience. The finished product exceeded academic expectations, displaying how thoughtful changes in design can enhance visual presentation leading to improved brand perception.

Artifact 02

Freelance Comprehensive Editing Project

This project focused on editing a travel guide for Italy, ensuring the final document was accurate, reliable, and easy to navigate for real-world use. Designed for an audience beyond the classroom, the work prioritized clarity, credibility, and overall user experience. Through extensive research and verification, all content was carefully reviewed against reputable sources. Image accuracy was a key focus, with reverse image searches used to identify and correct mismatches between visuals and text, strengthening the guide’s credibility. The final product demonstrates how effective editing goes beyond checking for spelling errors, it involves corrections to improve readability, accuracy, and user trust. By refining both content and presentation, the guide became a more credible and user-friendly resource, resulting in a highly positive response from the client.

Artifact 03

Grant Proposal

This proposal was developed to support an Arizona Animal Welfare League mini-grant application, using persuasive writing and structured evidence to clearly communicate funding needs and potential impact. Designed with a specific audience in mind, the document aligns with the requirements of the Scarritt Foundation while maintaining the organization’s brand identity. Utilizing reliable sources the proposal focuses on the real-world issue of overcrowding in animal shelters, the proposal outlines a plan for a “Dog Day Out” program that demonstrates how targeted funding could resolve the current crisis. Although the plan is fictitious, the project reflects realistic challenges and showcases how research and effective communication can advocate for meaningful change.

Artifact 04

Personas and Storytelling

This project develops a complete persona and associated narrative to model user-centered communication. Centered around the character of Julia Sterns, the project translates demographic and professional insights into a realistic, relatable profile. Each element of the persona is intentionally justified, ensuring that traits and behaviors are grounded in research rather than assumption.The result demonstrates how well-crafted personas can aid in creating a more effective product that anticipates the needs of the audience.

Artifact 05

Report on Intellectual Property

This report outlines key distinctions in intellectual property and copyright coverage, including practical examples. It was designed to make complex legal concepts more approachable for creators, students, and small organizations.Intellectual property is imperative to consider when creating content for a brand. It protects the brand identity and prevents competitors from copying. This report exemplifies the importance of Intellectual Property by detailing what is and isn’t covered under copyright, what qualifies as infringement, and examples of those concepts. Research was the most important element when creating this artifact. Through research I was able to identify cases that reflect the definitions of copyright. In the presentation the Nosferatu legal debacle is displayed that even if two projects aka, Nosferatu and Dracula share very little similarity it can still be infringement.An idea cannot be copyrighted but the expression of the idea can be.
