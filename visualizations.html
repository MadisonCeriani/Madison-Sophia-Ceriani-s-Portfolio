Artifact 01

Social Media Marketing Campaign

This group social media campaign strategy for Low Group outlines platform-specific content, messaging priorities, and engagement goals designed to strengthen brand presence on social media, and audience connection. The campaign delivers promotional materials tailored to the client’s needs, supporting clear marketing objectives. While collaborating, the team translated the client’s needs into targeted, thoughtfully designed content that aligned with both brand identity and audience expectations. Despite unexpected delays during the project timeline, my team adapted quickly and restructured our workflow to meet deadlines without compromising quality. The finished project successfully met both client and academic expectations, demonstrating the importance of flexibility, communication, and teamwork in delivering strong results under less than ideal circumstances.

Artifact 02

Tableau Dashboard

This data storytelling dashboard combines maps, charts, and contextual narrative to present complex trends in a clear and accessible format. By transforming credible data into intuitive visualizations, the project enables users to quickly identify patterns and gain meaningful insights. Designed with a strong focus on readability and clarity, the dashboard features multiple visualization types in an effective manner supporting a cohesive and engaging data story. Artifact 03

Book Cover Design

This Book Cover Project for Pet Sematary by Stephen King uses typography, mood, and minimal visual elements to evoke a sense of suspense and foreshadow the story’s darker themes. Through a bold minimalist visual approach, the design relies on subtle yet meaningful details to communicate tone. Red line elements resembling cat scratches foreshadow the dark events of the book, contrasting with childlike typography that reflects the role of naivety and innocence in the story’s progression. Overall, the design demonstrates that one can still accomplish an effective design with minimal elements when those elements are chosen thoughtfully.

Artifact 04

Usability Study Poster

This Research Poster, created for Arizona State University’s CISA student showcase, presents a usability study through a clear, presentation-style layout that highlights research methodology, key findings, and actionable recommendations. Designed to maximize readability, the poster translates research into a visually engaging power point ensuring clarity and easy comprehension. The strategic use of color drawn from ASU’s brand palette guides attention to key insights creating a clear visual hierarchy. This approach demonstrates how color can aid in the effective communication of complex information.The study evaluates the effectiveness of a bookstore’s design, revealing that improved signage could increase customer retention. By connecting research findings to practical recommendations, the project highlights the importance of usability studies and their practical applications.
